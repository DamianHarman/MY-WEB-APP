AI BATTLE
Card 1
Card 2
Cards are fixed for the entire battle. The same hole cards and the same 5 community cards are used for every hand — no card variance.

Only the betting strategies evolve. Each player uses Q-learning to gradually learn better fold / call / raise decisions for that specific board. Any chip movement is purely from strategy, not luck.

ε (epsilon) is the exploration rate — how often a player makes a random decision instead of using what they've learned. Starts at 0.40 and decays each hand down to a minimum of 0.05. A low ε means the player is mostly exploiting their learned strategy rather than experimenting.

raise× is the raise size multiplier. Starts at 0.75 and adapts over time — if raising wins, it nudges up (bet bigger); if raising loses, it nudges down (bet smaller). The Q-table decides whether to raise; raise× decides how much.

To find the ultimate winning strategy, run a battle then hit Continue Learning in the results. It automatically runs as many generations as needed — calculating the exact number of hands required each time — and stops only when all active players reach ε = 0.05. No manual clicking between generations.

The Learned Strategy heatmap appears directly below the final standings. Each cell shows the preferred action (F=Fold, C=Call, R=Raise) for a given hand strength and pot size. When fully converged, this is the ultimate strategy for those fixed cards.

The 5 columns represent hand strength — Weak (high card), Fair (one pair), Med (two pair), Good (trips/straight/flush), Strong (full house/quads/straight flush). The 3 dots within each cell represent pot size: small · med · big. Each cell answers: given this hand strength and pot size, what is the optimal action?
